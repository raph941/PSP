About "How Friends Shaped My Life"

To build the world, you need nations; to build communities you need people and to build people, you need friends. Friendship has a huge impact in the well being many people virtually more than anything. Having social connects and interactions improve lives and are even tied to longevity. Friendship transcends the click of a button to ‘add friends’ on a social media network. Our most powerful connections are the ones we stay in touch with in the real world.

The Book

This book is a captivating, interesting and appealing narrative; it is a memoir that is based personal reflections on friendships between the author and many people, events and places. It seeks to redefine what friendship actually means as a knowledge gap and how it should be perceived. Just as much as a gardener works to maintain the beauty of plants, outdoor grounds and trees in a garden, you will find in it words that seek to encourage the habit of maintaining positive and fruitful friendships. The author pulls together a wide collection of people via memories, true life experiences and insights thereby exploring friendship in a thoughtful and engaging way

Why "How Friends Shaped My Life"

To offer you a unique and valuable outlook on friendship, and redefine the true meaning and worth of human relationships.

The Mission.

  1. Socially connect thousands of people and positively transform its readers.
  2. Make a great difference by broadening the reader’s perspectives on friendship.
  3. Connect the past, present and future by passing information, wisdom and knowledge to others.

Author

Atomen Edom was born in Jos on the 24th of April 1980. He hails from Nasarawa State and is Eggon by tribe. He attended Police Children School in Jos, graduated in 1991 and completed his secondary school education at Government Science School, Kuru in Plateau State in 1997. He obtained a Bachelor of Science degree in 2004 and a Master of Science degree in 2008 in Architecture with MPHIL/PH.D Architecture (in view) from the prestigious University of Jos, Plateau State, Nigeria.
